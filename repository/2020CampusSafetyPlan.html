    2020 Campus Safety Plan

    Availability & Location of Law Enformacement Personnel and the Methods for Summoning Assistance

    Enforcement and Arrest Authority

    The California State Polytechnic University, Pomona (Cal Poly Pomona) University Police Department (UPD) is located at 3801 W. Temple Ave. Building 109, Pomona, CA 91768 and is available and provides law enforcement and police services to the campus community 24 hours a day, year-round. University Police Dispatch may be reached by phone at (909) 869-3070.

    Cal Poly Pomona UPD sworn peace officers are vested with full arrest and state-wide police authority and jurisdiction per California Penal Code section 830.2(c) and Education Code section 89560.  UPD’s primary jurisdiction includes all on campus properties owned and operated by Cal Poly Pomona and its auxiliaries and a one-mile radius from the campus.

    UPD police officers meet all of the requirements specified by the California Peace Officer Standards and Training Commission (POST), which are mandated for all sworn California law enforcement officers. UPS police officers are vested with law enforcement powers and responsibilities identical to local police and sheriff departments adjacent to campus and in your home community.   

    Crime Reporting

    The campus community is strongly encouraged to report all known or suspected crimes, and in-progress suspicious behavior or criminal activity, on campus to the University Police Department (UPD) as soon as possible.

    When reporting crimes, suspicious activity, or emergencies to UPD always:
    • Provide your name, telephone number, and location
    • Provide clear, accurate, detailed information
    • Be prepared to provide suspect and vehicle description, and direction of travel
    • Follow the Dispatcher’s instructions and stay on the line if it is safe to do so

    By Telephone Contact
    • Emergency: Dial 9-1-1 from campus landline; (909) 869-3070 from cell phone on campus
    • University Police Department: (909) 869-3070

    Emergency Blue Light Call Stations
    Blue light telephones are 9-1-1, 24-hour, button activated call stations that directly contact the police dispatcher for any emergency need. There are (98) call stations on campus, including the Village and Innovation Village.
    In Person Contact
    Building 109 is located on the corner of Cypress Lane and Oak Lane adjacent to Parking Structure 1.

    Emergency Hotline
    Information and updates will be provided on the university’s Emergency Hotline at (866) 869-POLY (7659). The campus community is encouraged to add this number to their contact lists.

    Special Safeguards for Facilities or Activities

    Security of and Access to Campus Facilities

    All campus facilities have key or security card access and most are accessible during business hours. Facilities Management is responsible for key control and distribution and may be reached at (909) 869-3030. The University Police Department (UPD) and Facilities Management jointly manage and service campus alarm and card access control systems.

    Access to on-campus student housing facilities is restricted to housing personnel, residents, their approved and escorted guests, and other authorized individuals. Housing security includes both key entry and card access.

    To provide for the security of campus facilities, UPD enforces Education Code 89031 and Housing Policies. Campus facility access may be revoked per Penal Code 626. Security cameras are located throughout the campus and may be monitored and/or utilized by the University Police Department for investigative purposes.

    Authorized individuals who need assistance accessing or security a campus building or office after hours should contact UPD at (909) 869-3070. Special access or after-hours access to campus facilities may be provided by UPD with prior campus authorization and proper identification.

    Access to exclusive or reserved use of campus facilities, buildings or spaces requires prior campus approval and advance scheduling. Access to and use of Cal Poly Pomona facilities, buildings, or outdoor spaces by non-campus entities requires prior authorization. For more information, contact Lease of Facilities at (909) 869-3030. 

    Special Event Safety Planning

    Preparation for large campus events involves the creation of Incident Action Plans according to the Standardized Emergency Management system and in collaboration with mutual aid agencies and contracted safety personnel.

    New Student Orientations

    University Police personnel provide personal safety tips and emergency contact information to new students at orientations. Students are informed of policies, crime reporting procedures and safety programs through regular outreach through email, social media, and event tabling throughout the academic year.

    New Employee Orientations

    All new employees receive information regarding safety policies, procedures and view the Emergency Management safety video as part of their new employee orientation with Human Resource Services.

    Actions in the Last 18 Months to Increase Safety

    • Conducted active shooter exercise to enhance police preparedness (Spring 2018)
    • Conducted campus wide evacuation exercise with Emergency Operations Center activation and University Police coordinated response in conjunction with Great California Shakeout (Fall 2019)
    • Operationalized cross-divisional Critical Incident Team spearheaded by the Student Affairs Division
    • Hired additional police officers and full-time community service officers to enhance campus safety
    • Conducted enhanced training academy for evacuation coordinators (emergency response volunteers)
    • Upgraded campus fire alarm system for improved operability

    Changes in Safety Precautions During the Next 24 Months

    • Continue to expand and enhance video systems, equipment, and capabilities campus wide
    • Implement mapping and text to 9-1-1 for alternative emergency contact to University Police Dispatch Center
    • Complete Pedestrian Safety Upgrade Project, including installation of sidewalks and speed control measures on Eucalyptus Lane
    • Publish University Police Department policies for public access
    • Conduct comprehensive assessment of campus building access and security systems and blue
    • Continue to enhance campus evacuation plan and evacuation coordinator program
    • Develop Faculty/Staff Crisis Response manual spearheaded by the Student Affairs Division
    • Evaluate campus blue phone locations and operability to improve accessibility and visibility
    • Increase outreach to the campus community regarding police protocols and response

    California State University, Pomona Statistical Report 2018

    The report is a separate and distinct report from the Annual Security Report required under the Jeanne Clery Disclosure of Campus Security Policy and Campus Crime Statistics Act (the Clery Act). The Clery Act report may be found online at http://www.cpp.edu/pdfs/annual_security_report.pdf

    These statistics were compiled using the FBI Uniform Crime Reporting program definitions, occurring on campus, and in compliance with the California State Education Code, Chapter 16, of the Donahue Higher Education Act, Section 67380.
