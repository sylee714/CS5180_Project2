    2020 Hunger and Homelessness Awareness Week

    November 15th - November 21st

    Join us for a week of programming focused on student hunger and homelessness. Hunger and Homelessness Awareness Week is designed to educate the public, draw attention to the problem of poverty and build up the base of volunteers and supporters for local anti-poverty agencies. Follow Basic Needs on Instagram for updates and more educational resources! 

    This event is brought to you by the Cal Poly Pomona Basic Needs Task Force. 

    Bronco Basic Needs Kit

    Students can register for a basic needs kit to support with hygiene, PPE and other essential items related to basic needs. Kits will be mailed out directly to students. 

    Receive Your Kit

    Virtual Events

    Supporting Basic Needs in the California State University

    Tuesday, Nov. 17th - 12 pm to 1 pm

    Hosted by the CSU Chancellor's Office, this webinar includes a moderated panel discussion to highlight research that has informed the ways by which students’ basic needs are supported. Resources will be shared, as well as recommendations for faculty and staff on supporting students in accessing critical supports. Panelists will also highlight innovative programs and services across the 23 campuses aimed to support student success.

    Register Here

    Hungry to Learn: Documentary Viewing & Dinner

    Wednesday, Nov. 18th - 5 pm to 7 pm

    Join us for a viewing of the powerful documentary “Hungry to Learn,” produced by Soledad O’Brien and Rose Acre. This is the story of how colleges, once places for children of privilege, opened their doors to students of limited means but failed to provide enough financial aid to allow these new students to graduate without making painful choices.

    GrubHub gift cards will be provided to the first 100 students who register and watch the documentary.

    Register Here

    My Story: Understanding Student Hunger & Homelessness Panel

    Thursday, Nov. 19th - 12 pm to 1 pm

    Learn about CSU and campus efforts to address food and housing insecurity and hear from CPP students and staff/faculty how they have navigated student hunger and homelessness through a moderated panel.

    GrubHub gift cards will be provided to the first 100 students who register and attend the panel.

    Register Here

    Get Involved

    Thanksgiving Dinner

    Donate or gift a Thanksgiving dinner to a student or employee in need this year. For each complete dinner purchased, donated or gifted, CPP Dining will donate TWO single serving meals to a student in need, and you will be entered in a raffle to win a CPP gift bag full of Bronco Gear! 

    Order or Donate 

    Students, faculty or staff who have been impacted by the COVID-19 pandemic or other unexpected hardship can apply for a free Thanksgiving dinner package.

    Learn More

    Feed a Bronco: Swipe Out Hunger

    Students with a meal plan will be able to donate a meal to another student in need through the Broncos Care Meal Sharing Program. Residential students on campus will receive more information.

    Stock the Poly Pantry: Amazon Wish List

    Donate toiletries, non-perishable food and nutritious snacks by simply clicking below!
