    Uncharted Horizons Podcast

    Uncharted Horizons is the official podcast of the Learn Through Discovery Initiative - where the goal is that every student at Cal Poly Pomona will have at least one Signature Polytechnic Experience (PolyX) before graduation. PolyX are a hands-on approach to learning where students use their knowledge and skills to create a meaningful impact. PolyX almost always involve research, innovation, and service.

    Season 1 of Uncharted Horizons (2019-2020) explores what research means for different disciplines and how students can get involved. Season 2 of Uncharted Horizons (2020-2021) explores innovation across the Cal Poly Pomona campus. Season 3 (coming 2021-2022) will explore community service.

    Guests on the podcast include diverse members of the CPP community. Podcasts are released multiple times per semester in our monthly LTD Newsletter.

    Episode 1

    Dr. Kate Ozment

    Episode 1

    This episode features Dr. Kate Ozment, Assistant Professor in the English and Modern Languages Department at Cal Poly Pomona. Dr. Ozment works on women’s writing and text technologies, focusing on how re-creating historical printing practices helps us learn more about the interplay of manuscript, print, and digital technologies in our modern world. Her research is currently investigating how women book collectors have shaped the study of material artifacts in the United States in the mid-twentieth century. These women were unable to attend most universities, but they used book collecting as self-education and to build reputations as scholarly and knowledgeable despite gendered gatekeeping in academia. In Dr. Ozment’s teaching, she incorporates thinking about how literary histories are formed by libraries and archives, and asks students to take on active roles in telling these stories by becoming the storytellers themselves with public-facing writing, creative pieces, and turning courses into RPG quests.

    Episode 8

    Dr. Zahra Sotoudeh

    Episode 8

    This episode features Dr. Zahra Sotoudeh, an Assistant Professor of Aerospace Engineering at Cal Poly Pomona.  Dr. Sotoudeh has published two books and 12 journal articles, has held several leadership positions in her field of study, and has implemented new pedagogies like active learning techniques and flipped classrooms in the Aerospace Engineering Department. Her PhD specializes in computational nonlinear aeroelasticity and her research interests are in developing computational methods for studying the vibrational behavior of structures in a wide range of frequencies. She has developed programs and methods using Finite Element Method (FEM) and Statistical Energy Analysis (SEA) to study high-aspect-ratio flying wings, and helicopter rotor blades structural dynamics. She has also studied energy harvesting from smart material.

    Episode 7

    Dr. Peter Arensburger

    Episode 7

    This episode features Dr. Peter Arensburger, Assistant Professor in the Biological Sciences Department at Cal Poly Pomona. Dr. Arensburger works at the interface between molecular biology and computer science. As a bioinformatician his research involves using computer program to search insect and spider DNA genomes for new patterns and features. One such feature are “transposable elements," DNA sequences outside of genes that are able move around the genome, inserting themselves into new locations. Understanding how transposable elements are able to do this, as well as describing the variety and diversity of such elements (there are a lot of them) is an important step in genetic engineering and understanding how genomes are structured.

    Jennette Ramirez

    Episode 6

    This episode features Jennette Ramirez, a Cal Poly Pomona alumna (Class of 2019) who earned her degree in Gender, Ethnic, Multicultural Studies (GEMS). While at Cal Poly Pomona, she spent her time working at The Pride Center and conducting research as a McNair Scholar. She will be attending the University of Southern California this fall, pursuing a Ph.D. in American Studies and Ethnicity. Her research focuses on counternarratives of state violence using her developing Beautiful Monster Theory. Jennette is also an active member of the community, working as the Social Media Director for the Pomona Community Farmers Alliance, a food justice and community-centered collective in the local area.

    Episode 5

    Janam Dave

    Episode 5

    This episode features Janam Dave, an MS graduate student in the Biological Sciences department at Cal Poly Pomona. As an undergraduate student, he double majored in Chemical Engineering and Biotechnology at CPP (Class of 2018). His research with faculty mentor Dr. Adler-Moore focuses on finding better treatment options for invasive pulmonary aspergillosis, a serious lung disease that currently causes 50-90% mortality in patients. Janam is investigating aerosol drug delivery as a treatment option and built an aerosol delivery system that can help generate small, inhalable drug particles that reach the deepest parts of the lung. His research indicates that this form of treatment is promising and may reduce the high rate of mortality seen in patients. Recently, Janam earned first place at the 2019 California State Research Competition for his MS thesis research. He looks forward to completing this work, graduating with his MS this year (2020), and then pursuing a PhD in Biology.

    Episode 4

    Dr. Jessie Vallejo

    This episode features Dr. Jessie Vallejo, an Assistant Professor in Ethnomusicology and Director of Mariachi Ensembles in Cal Poly Pomona's Music Department. Her research focuses on a hemispheric approach to Amerindian studies, Indigenous cultural-linguistic revitalization projects, and mariachi music. Dr. Vallejo has also co-produced, annotated, and provided photography for Smithsonian Folkways’ 2013 release ¡Así Kotama!: The Flutes of Otavalo, Ecuador. In addition to her research and teaching, Jessie is an active freelance violinist in the Los Angeles area. She received her Ph.D. from UCLA’s Ethnomusicology Department in 2014.
